Project Context

A safety-critical composite component, designed and raced.

I contributed to the development of Polimi Motorcycle Factory's first carbon-fiber swingarm: a unique structural component designed, manufactured and raced by the university team.

The first generation combined a carbon-fiber structure with aluminium inserts and weighed approximately 3 kg. Its laminate was designed around load cases derived from the static tests required for the motorcycle.

For the following motorcycle, the external geometry was retained while the internal architecture and plybook were redesigned to improve structural efficiency and reduce the use of aluminium components.

Approach

Design, simulation, and manufacturing moved together.

The project was developed through an iterative design-simulation-manufacturing process.

I contributed to translating the motorcycle's static and operational loads into structural load cases, optimizing the composite laminate through Abaqus and Isight, and defining the production plybook.

The factor of safety was considered as a key design requirement throughout the structural assessment, helping guide laminate choices for a safety-critical racing component.

I also contributed to the design and manufacturing of the carbon-fiber molds used to produce the component.

For the second generation, the experience gained from the first prototype, manufactured in three units, was applied to improve both the design and the production process. Unidirectional carbon fiber was introduced, and the use of a Rohacell insert was successfully validated. The manufacturing process also demonstrated that it was possible to produce a more complex component with lower stiffness but the same load-carrying capability, while significantly reducing its weight.

Evidence

Manufactured, assembled, and validated in competition.

Both swingarm generations were successfully manufactured, assembled and validated in competition.

The components were used on motorcycles that achieved second place in the university competition and multiple podium finishes in the Italian CIV championship.

The project covered the complete development of a safety-critical racing component: from load-case definition and composite optimization to tooling, manufacturing, motorcycle integration and track use.
